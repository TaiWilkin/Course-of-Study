Language: Grammar and Composition

This is a free course in language. "Language" refers the study of grammar and composition. At this level, this is done orally in short lessons. It's recommended that you complete a mini-lesson (from Part I) at the beginning of the week, and then take advantage of naturally-arising opportunities throughout the week to reinforce the concept.

Sample stories for models are given in Part II, to assist the teacher with the later work of the year. In Part III, short retellings of fables are given. Each week, read one fable to your student and ask them to then tell you the same story in their own words.

Arithmetic

This is a free course in arithmetic. Expect this textbook to take two years to complete. Aim to complete lessons one through twenty-three this year.

Read the "Notes to the Teacher" carefully. For each lesson, plan to spend a week using manipulatives (pencils, coins, pebbles, etc.) to master the concept being taught in the lesson, before moving to the book for the second week. Create numerous additional problems of the same type and level as those given in the book until you are certain your student has mastered the skill.

It is better to move slowly and thoroughly than to move quickly and without mastery.

Literature, History and Civics

This is a free course in literature and history, focusing on reading and analyzing classic children's literature and poetry, with weekly readings about famous historical figures.

In order to provide quality content in large quantities for free, the books in this course are adapted from out-of-copyright vintage textbooks, which in their original format are severely lacking in diverse representation. To broaden the historical coverage, brand-new biographies have been written to cover a more diverse population.

However, I have been unable to source many examples of free children's literature which is written by diverse authors. A list of modern children's literature is included, and it is highly recommended that you supplement the provided lessons with read-alouds of as many of these as you are able to obtain. You should be able to find many of them in your local library.
